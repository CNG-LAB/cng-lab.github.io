Our group

Interested in joining us for a short or longer while? Please get in touch wiith me; valk [at] cbs [dot] mpg [dot] de or s [dot] valk [at] fz-juelich [dot] de.



Sofie Valk

Research group leader

Sofie is Otto Hahn research group leader at the MPI in Leipzig, Germany and research group leader at INM-7 Juelich, Juelich, Germany. Together with her research group she studies interplay of structure and functon in the human brain.

Lina Schaare

Postdoc

Lina has a background in psychology and cognitive neuroscience. Her research explores how effects of health-related measures shape brain structure and function, what underlies these effects (neuronal vs. non-neuronal contributions), and how they are driven by environmental and genetic influences.

Şeyma Bayrak

PhD candidate

Şeyma studies the functional and structural organisation of the human brain. She loves big data and computational challenges and recently the functional neurosurgery.

Bin Wan

PhD candidate, IMPRS

Bin is studying asymmetry of brain organization. In parallel, he has wide interests in how the human mind (from behavior, ideology to brain circuits) is shaped by nature and nurture. Personal website.

Amin Saberi

PhD candidate, hiball

Amin studies the link between excitation-inhibition and cortical structure, and their development in typically developing adolescents and those at higher risk for psychopathology.

Meike Hettwer

PhD candidate, School of Cognition

Meike is a PhD student at Max Planck School of Cognition. Her work aims to understand how brain organization shapes resilience and vulnerability in mental health, in the larger context of transdiagnostic psychiatry and network-based pathology.

Neville Magielse

PhD candidate, hiball

Neville just started on his PhD on the cognitive function of the cerebellum. He will consider this from different perspectives, by looking at cerebellar evolution, microstructure, and functional imaging.

Svenja Küchenhoff

PhD candidate, hiball

Svenja links ex-vivo ultra-high resolution cytoarchitectural data to in-vivo imaging data to bridge the gap between micro- and macro-scale neuroscience. She focusses on organizing principles of plastic brain structures. Svenja previously studied neuroscience, cognitive sciences, and psychology.

Benjamin Hanisch

MD PhD candidate

Ben studies the principles of neurotransmitter transporter and receptor molecule distribution in the brain and their link to hallmarks of brain function and disease, hoping to better understand the genesis and treatment of psychiatric illnesses.

Yun Shuang Fan

visiting PhD

Yun Shuang is visiting the lab through the Chinese State-Sponsored Scholarship for joint PhD students program. She works on various projects bridging brain organization and Schizophrenia.

Giacomo Bignardi

PhD guest, School of Cognition

Giaco(mo) was born in the humanities but was raised by scientists. All grown up, he is trying to accept both past, pursuing a PhD on the biological basis of aesthetics with the Max Planck School of Cognition at the Language and Genetics Department of the MPI for psycholinguistics, in Nijmegen, while being a nomadic guest of the Frankfurt and Leipzig MPIs.

Pepijn Meurs

MSc intern

Pepijn is a master student at the University of Amsterdam, where he studies Brain and Society. He is working on the interrelation between the vascular system and brain structure, and co-organizing various outreach activities in the scope of brain awareness week during his internship with us.

Eliaou Balouka

Erasmus exchange

Eliaou is a clinical psychologist, interested in bridging basic cognitive neuroscience and clinical practise. In our lab he is studying the interrelationship between clinical practise and basic research, and learning various techniques to map the brain.

Alex John

MSc candidate

Alex comes from a biology background and is in the final phase of her master’s studies with focus on neurobiology and behavioral science at the University of Leipzig. Currently she is writing her master’s thesis in our lab.
